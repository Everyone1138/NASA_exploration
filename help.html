Search history:

Welcome to our astronomical page! Click on the Search/History button to begin!

Once you click the ‘search/history’ button on the top left hand corner of the page you’ll see a side menu pop out.

In that menu you are presented with some options:

  • Home
  • The team
  • Help

The home button will take you back to the home page. This will be helpful if you want to do a new search. Below it is The team button. If you click on that you can see all the awesome people that worked on this site!

The Help button takes you to this page.

The Search Bar and Fields(Search Criteria!)

Here is a description of each search field to help you better understand how these fields benefit your search.

  • Reguired: Description
  • Optional: Keywords, Titles, Start Year, End Year

In the "Required" field you'll want to put in the name/title of the space or NASA related topic you're looking for.

For example: Planet Earth. The Milky Way, Apollo 11, etc. The Optional fields can be left blank. And if you just fill in one of those fields and either hit 'enter' or click on 'search criteria above', a search will be produced. What you get in return an image from the NASA api.

Wikipedia Search Bar!

Now that you've found an image on the topic you're doing research on. Now you can look up more information on said topic

In the search field you can retype the name of the astronomical topic you're looking for. Once you do you'll see information come up. If you need more information you can click on the text and it'll take you to the corresponding Wikipedia page. If you click on 'Reset your Wikipedia search HERE' it will reset your search for you in case you need to look something else up!
